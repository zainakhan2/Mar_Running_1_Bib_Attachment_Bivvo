10 Common Race Day Mistakes (And How to Avoid Every One)

~1,800 words · Target: "race day mistakes to avoid marathon" · Publish: Day 240

After talking to hundreds of runners, these are the 10 mistakes that ruin race day the most often. All are preventable.

1. Going Out Too Fast

The #1 race mistake. Adrenaline makes your first mile feel easy. By mile 18, you pay for it. Run your first mile 10-15 seconds SLOWER than goal pace.

2. New Shoes on Race Day

Never. Wear shoes with 30-50 miles on them. Broken in but not worn out.

3. Skipping Body Glide

Chafing doesn't start until mile 8-10. By then it's too late. Apply to inner thighs, armpits, nipples, and any bra/waistband contact points BEFORE the race.

4. Wrong Fueling

Eat 2-3 hours before. Nothing new. Your pre-race meal should be tested in training at least 3 times.

5. Fumbling with Safety Pins

Cold fingers + tiny pins + dark parking lot = race morning misery. Attach your bib the night before with bib clips. Problem solved in 12 seconds.

6. Ignoring the Weather

Check the forecast the morning of. Dress for 15-20°F warmer than the temperature (running generates significant heat).

7. Not Using the Bathroom

Go early. Go often. The porta-potty line at T-minus-10 is a disaster. Budget 20 minutes.

8. Starting Your Watch Late

GPS needs time to lock satellites. Start your watch 2-3 minutes before the gun.

9. No Post-Race Plan

Where's your bag? Where did you park? Who's meeting you? Plan the after before the during.

10. Comparing Yourself to Others

Run YOUR race. Not the person next to you. Not your training partner. Your pace. Your plan. Your day.
