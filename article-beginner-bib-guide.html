First Race? A Beginner's Guide to Race Bibs, Timing Chips, and Start Lines

~1,800 words · Target: "first race bib guide beginner" · Publish: Day 120

Your first road race is exciting — and a little overwhelming. You've trained, you've got your shoes, and you just picked up your race bib at packet pickup. Now what?

What's in Your Race Packet

Race bib: A paper or Tyvek number that identifies you. It has your name, number, and sometimes a timing chip attached. You must wear it visibly on the front of your shirt during the race.

Timing chip: A small transponder (either attached to your bib or given separately for your shoe/ankle). This records your start time, splits, and finish time when you cross timing mats on the course.

Safety pins: Most races give you 4 safety pins to attach your bib. These work, but they poke holes in your shirt and are tricky with cold fingers. Many experienced runners now use pinless bib clips instead.

How to Attach Your Race Bib

Attach your bib to the FRONT of your race shirt, below chest height. Use all 4 corners for a secure hold. The number should be clearly visible — race officials and photographers need to see it.

Pro tip: Attach your bib the night before. One less thing to do at 5:30 AM in a dark parking lot.

Race Morning Timeline

3 hours before: Eat your pre-race breakfast (toast, banana, coffee).

2 hours before: Start sipping water. Stop 30 minutes before the start.

1 hour before: Arrive at the venue. Find parking. Locate your corral.

30 minutes before: Bathroom (the line will be long). Apply Body Glide.

10 minutes before: Light dynamic warm-up. Get into your corral.

Start: Don't go out too fast. First mile should feel easy. You'll thank yourself at mile 10.

Common First-Race Mistakes

  • Going out too fast (the adrenaline is real)
  • Wearing brand-new shoes or clothes (test everything in training first)
  • Skipping the bathroom before the start
  • Not eating early enough (stomach needs 2-3 hours to settle)
  • Forgetting to start your GPS watch
