Triathlon Transition Zone Hacks: Shave Minutes Off Your T1 and T2

~1,600 words · Target: "triathlon transition zone tips T1 T2" · Publish: Day 180

In triathlon, the race happens in the transition zone as much as on the course. Here are the hacks that experienced triathletes use to shave minutes — not just seconds — off their T1 and T2 times.

T1: Swim → Bike

Pre-stage everything: Helmet open on handlebars, sunglasses inside helmet, shoes pre-clipped to pedals (advanced), towel for sandy/wet feet.

Elastic laces on bike shoes: If not clipped in, use elastic laces for slip-on entry.

Practice the wetsuit strip: The fastest method is to unzip while running from swim exit, pull arms out, roll to waist, step-step-pull in one motion.

T2: Bike → Run

Pre-attach your race bib: Use twist-lock bib clips to attach your bib to your run top the night before. Lay the shirt in T2 ready to go. Total transition time for bib: 0 seconds.

Run shoes with elastic laces: Lock Laces or similar. Slip in, pull tab, go. No tying.

Visor on top of run shoes: Grab and go. Don't search.

The 5-Item T2 Layout

Left to right on your towel: (1) Run shoes with elastic laces, (2) Race belt or pre-bibbed shirt, (3) Nutrition for the run, (4) Sunglasses, (5) Visor/hat. Nothing else. Minimalism wins in T2.
