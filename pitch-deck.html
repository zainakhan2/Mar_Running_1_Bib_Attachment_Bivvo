The Problem

18.1 million US race finishers annually attach their bibs with safety pins — a 50-year-old method that punches holes in expensive jerseys, pokes fingers in the cold, and gets dropped in wet grass at 5:30 AM.

Magnetic clips emerged as an alternative but carry a theoretical risk of interfering with RFID timing chips — a deal-breaker for competitive runners chasing qualifying times and age group awards.

No mechanical (non-magnetic) pinless solution exists at consumer scale. The #1 race-morning complaint has no proper answer.

The Solution

BIVVO is a twist-lock (bayonet-mount) race bib clip. The same mechanical principle as a camera lens mount — proven in millions of devices worldwide — adapted for race bib attachment.

Insert. Twist. Click.

Push through bib + shirt → twist 90° → hear the click. 3 seconds per clip. 12 seconds total. Zero magnets. Zero pins. Zero holes.
