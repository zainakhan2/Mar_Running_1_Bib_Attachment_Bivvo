Race Expo Survival Guide: How to Navigate a Marathon Expo Without Going Broke

~1,600 words · Target: "marathon expo tips guide" · Publish: Day 300

Marathon expos are overwhelming — hundreds of booths, free samples everywhere, and that nagging feeling that you NEED to buy something. Here's how to navigate like a pro.

Before You Go

Set a budget. $20-$50 is reasonable. Anything you buy at the expo you could probably find cheaper online later. The exception: expo-exclusive race merchandise (shirts, jackets) that sell out.

Eat beforehand. You'll sample energy gels and bars until you're nauseous. A full stomach helps you resist the free sample gauntlet.

Must-Do at Every Expo

Pick up your bib + timing chip. That's why you're here. Do this FIRST. Lines get longer as the day goes on.

Check your bib info. Verify your name, age group, corral assignment, and timing chip number. Errors found at the expo are fixable. Errors found on race morning are not.

Buy race-day essentials you forgot. Body Glide, bib clips, energy gels, sunscreen. The expo is your last chance to buy these before race morning.

Skip These

New shoes. You should never race in shoes you haven't trained in. Just say no to the tempting expo shoe deals.

New clothing. Same rule. Nothing new on race day. If you want the expo hoodie, buy it for training — not racing.

Overpriced race photos. Wait until after the race to see if your photos are worth buying. Pre-paying rarely makes sense.

Hidden Gems

The best finds at expos are small accessories: bib clips, running socks, anti-chafe products, hydration supplements, and race pace bands. These are the products that actually improve your race day — not another dri-fit shirt you don't need.
