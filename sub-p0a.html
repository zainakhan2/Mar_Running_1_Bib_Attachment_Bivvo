Runner Demographics

18.1 million US road race finishers annually (Running USA 2023). 60% run 3+ races/year. Average 4.2 races/year for repeat runners. Peak seasons: spring (March–May) and fall (September–November) marathon series.

Target persona: recreational competitive runner, ages 28–55, runs 3+ races/year, owns $200+ in running gear, active on Strava/Instagram, willing to pay for convenience products that protect expensive gear.

Secondary persona: triathlete (2.5M US participants), needs rapid bib transitions in T1/T2 zones. Race belt alternative — clips stay on the jersey, no belt needed.

Market Trends

  • Running participation rebounded 14% post-COVID (2022–2024). 5K entries up 22%.
  • Premium running gear spending up 18% YoY. Runners increasingly invest in accessories.
  • Safety pin complaints consistently rank top-3 in race-day feedback surveys.
  • Magnetic clip adoption growing but timing chip concern creates market headroom for non-magnetic alternatives.
  • Amazon search volume for 'race bib clips' up 35% in 2024 vs 2023.
