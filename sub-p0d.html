P0D: Patent & IP Landscape Analysis

Freedom-to-operate analysis covering 15+ existing patents in the bib clip and bayonet-mount space.

Patent Search Results

Searched USPTO, EPO, and WIPO databases for: race bib clip, bib attachment device, magnetic bib holder, bayonet mount clip, twist-lock fastener, garment attachment clip.

15+ relevant patents reviewed. Key findings:

  • US9907365B2 (2018) — Magnetic bib attachment system. Covers magnetic approach only. Does NOT block mechanical/twist-lock.
  • US10448693B2 (2019) — Snap-together bib clip. Covers snap mechanism only. Different mechanical principle from bayonet.
  • US20200100567A1 (2020) — Friction-fit bib holder. Published application, not granted. Friction ≠ twist-lock.
  • Multiple bayonet-mount patents exist in INDUSTRIAL applications (camera mounts, lens systems, pipe fittings) but NONE in garment/bib attachment.
  • No blocking patents found for bayonet-mount mechanism applied to race bib attachment.

Freedom to Operate

FTO Assessment: GREEN — Clear to proceed.

No existing patent covers a bayonet-mount (twist-lock) mechanism applied to garment/bib attachment. The closest patents cover magnetic systems, snap systems, and industrial bayonet mounts (camera/lens) — none of which overlap with BIVVO's specific application.

Recommendation: File utility patent application before first public sale to establish priority date. Use IP Accelerator attorney for both trademark and patent to coordinate filings.
