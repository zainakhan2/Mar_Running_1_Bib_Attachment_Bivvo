Why Twist-Lock Won

Twist-lock scored highest (8.4/10) because it's the only mechanism that scores 8+ on BOTH hold strength (9) AND timing chip safety (10). Magnetic clips score well on ease-of-use (9) but their timing chip safety score (5) is a fatal weakness for competitive runners.

The bayonet-mount principle is proven in millions of camera lens mounts (Canon EF, Nikon F), lightbulb sockets, and garden hose fittings. Adapting this proven mechanical principle to race bib attachment is a novel application, not a novel mechanism — reducing engineering risk.

Scoring Weights

  • Hold Strength (20%): Must stay attached during running, jumping, rain
  • Fabric Safety (15%): Zero damage to race jerseys ($60–$150 value)
  • Timing Chip Safety (20%): CRITICAL — any magnetic interference is a deal-breaker
  • Ease of Use (15%): Must work in 3 seconds, cold fingers, dark conditions
  • Durability (10%): Target 500+ uses minimum
  • Aesthetics (10%): Low-profile, doesn't look industrial
  • Manufacturing Cost (10%): Must achieve $14.99 retail at healthy margins
